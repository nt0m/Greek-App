Some interesting things for you to know about the Greek Language


The Greek Alphabet has been used to write the Greek language since the late 9th century BC or early 8th century BC. It was the first alphabetic script to have distinct letters for vowels as well as consontants. It is the ancestor of the Latin and Cyrillic scripts. Apart from its use in writing the Greek language, in both its ancient and its modern forms, the Greek alphabet today also serves as a source of technical symbols and labels in many domains of mathematics, science and other fields.
